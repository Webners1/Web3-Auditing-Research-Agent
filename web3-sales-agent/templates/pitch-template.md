## Cold DM Structure Rules

1. **Never open with a compliment** — opens with a specific observation about their protocol
2. **One pain point maximum** — don't list everything wrong with their protocol in message 1
3. **One proof point** — the most relevant case study, in 1 sentence
4. **One ask** — specific (not "let me know if interested"), low-friction (not "can we get on a call?")
5. **Under 150 words** — they read on mobile, 3–4 sentences max

## Ghost Lead DM
```
Hey [team name / @handle],

I was looking at [Protocol] on Base — love the [specific mechanic, e.g., "concentrated liquidity design"] 
but noticed the UI is running on what looks like a [Uniswap fork / unresponsive mobile layout / 
stale Figma template], which tends to stop institutional wallets from engaging.

I rebuilt the frontend for [similar protocol] — their TVL went from $80k to $160k in 6 weeks after 
the UI launch. No new incentives, just removing the friction.

Would it be useful to see a quick before/after concept for your main swap flow?

— [Your name/ENS]
```

## Leaky Bucket DM
```
Hey [team name],

Noticed [Protocol]'s active wallets have dropped ~[X]% in the last 30 days on DappRadar 
while your TVL is holding. That's usually a UX problem — users are showing up but not completing 
the core action.

I ran your deposit/stake flow just now. There are [X] places where a new user would stop 
and not come back. Happy to show you exactly where.

Quick walk-through — 15 min — no commitment. Worth it?

— [Your name/ENS]
```

## Security Risk DM
```
Hey [team name],

Saw you announced [V2 / contract upgrade / new chain] launch for [Protocol]. 
Congrats — that's a big milestone.

One thing: the upgrade/migration window is when most DeFi exploits happen. 
With $[TVL] on-chain, finding a critical issue post-launch costs 10x what finding it pre-launch does.

I'll do a free 2-hour logic check on the new contracts before you ship — just looking for 
the high-severity stuff that's easy to miss when you're deep in the code. No strings.

DM if you want to coordinate.

— [Your name/ENS]
```

## Chain Migrator DM
```
Hey [team name],

Just saw [Protocol] launched on [chain] — nice to see more real DeFi activity there.

I've done [N] integrations on [chain] and the wallet detection + bridging UX is usually 
the part that kills the launch momentum. Most teams underestimate the [chain-specific issue: 
EIP-1559 differences / MetaMask chain switch prompt / custom RPC config].

Happy to compare notes on what I've seen go wrong — no pitch, just practical. 
What's your current wallet stack?

— [Your name/ENS]
```

## Email Subject Lines (Use One)
- "[Protocol] — quick question about your [specific feature] UX"
- "Noticed something on [Protocol]'s [mobile / TVL / active wallets]"
- "Before [Protocol]'s V2 ships — 2 hours that could matter"
- "Re: [Protocol]'s launch on [chain] — chain integration tip"

---

## What to NEVER Write in a First Pitch
- "I'd love to discuss how I can help your project..."
- "We are a leading provider of..."
- "Congratulations on your recent [achievement]..."  (sycophantic opener)
- "I noticed you are in the DeFi space..."  (too generic — shows you didn't look at their protocol)
- Attach a pitch deck or long proposal to a first cold DM
